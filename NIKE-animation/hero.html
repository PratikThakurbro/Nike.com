Nike is a major American multinational corporation that was founded in 1964 as Blue Ribbon Sports and officially became Nike, Inc. in 1971. Here's a brief overview of its history and how its iconic shoes were developed:
Founded: January 25, 1964, by Bill Bowerman and Phil Knight.
Original Name: Blue Ribbon Sports (BRS). The company started as a distributor for the Japanese shoemaker Onitsuka Tiger (now ASICS).
Rebranding: In 1971, the company rebranded as Nike, Inc., named after the Greek goddess of victory
First Shoe: The first Nike shoe, the Nike Cortez, was introduced in 1972. It was developed by Bill Bowerman, a track coach, who used innovative designs and materials to improve performance.
Waffle Sole: In 1971, Bill Bowerman created the Waffle Sole by pouring rubber into his wife’s waffle iron, which provided improved traction and cushioning. This design became a hallmark of Nike’s early running shoes.
Air Technology: Nike introduced Air Max technology in 1987 with the Air Max 1, which featured visible air cushioning in the sole for enhanced comfort and shock absorption.
Swoosh Logo: The iconic Swoosh logo was designed by graphic designer Carolyn Davidson in 1971. It symbolizes movement and speed.
Nike became known for its powerful marketing campaigns and endorsements with athletes like Michael Jordan. The Air Jordan line, launched in 1985, became one of the most famous and influential sneaker lines in history.
Innovations: Over the years, Nike has continued to innovate with various technologies, including Flyknit, React foam, and self-lacing shoes like the Nike Adapt.
Global Presence: Nike is now one of the largest and most recognized sportswear brands globally, known for its cutting-edge technology, stylish designs, and influential marketing strategies..
